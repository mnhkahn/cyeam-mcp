# Animation and Film Language

## Camera and Lenses

A wide-angle lens captures a broad field of view with exaggerated perspective and noticeable distortion. It is suited to large scenes, tight environments, and deliberate artistic effects.

Camera movements include: push (dolly in), pull (dolly out), pan, tilt, track, follow, whip, and combined motion shots.

## The Axis Rule

The axis is an imaginary line determined by an object's movement direction or facing. Cameras should be placed within a 180-degree arc on one side of the axis. Crossing the axis — called "crossing the line" — can disorient the viewer. Legitimate methods to cross the axis include:

- Cutaway shot
- On-axis shot
- Following motion shot
- Subjective point-of-view shot
- Close-up shot

Intentional axis crossing is also valid when an artistic effect is desired.

## Animation Principles

From a three-dimensional animation production standpoint, character design must balance artistic vision with engineering feasibility. The stages of 3D production include: modeling, texturing, rigging, animation, rendering, and post-production compositing.

Motion follows a sequence of phases: start, anticipation, action, and recovery.

## Film Language Elements

Motion in film divides into internal motion (movement within the frame) and external motion (camera movement). Sound consists of music, sound effects, and dialogue.
