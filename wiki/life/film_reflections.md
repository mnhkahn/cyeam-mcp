# Film Reflections

## *Lust, Caution*

In November 2013, the subject wrote an analysis of Ang Lee's film *Lust, Caution*, focusing on the final scene in which the protagonist Wang Jiazhi chooses not to take the suicide capsule prepared for agents.

The subject identified multiple sources of resentment that explained her decision:

- **The male student she loved in college** sent a classmate with brothel experience to train her in sexual matters rather than doing so himself. She hated his cowardice.
- **The six university students** who planned to assassinate a spy chief displayed dangerous naivety. She hated their ignorance.
- **Her handler** deceived her with promises of sending her to England and withheld her letters to her father. She hated the deception.
- **Her father** took her brother to England but left her behind, later remarrying and clearly wishing to keep her from disrupting his new family. Her aunt sold her only house just to pay for her university tuition.

The subject concluded that Wang Jiazhi's life had been a series of betrayals and abandonments. Having been stopped at the last moment from escaping, she saw herself as someone who "had a grudge against the world" and would not surrender easily. She wanted to live long enough to see the classmate she both hated and felt guilty toward.
