# Reading and Self-Discovery

## 2012 Reading List

In 2012, the subject read six books cover-to-cover, marking what they described as their first year of sustained extracurricular reading. The reading list spanned history, memoir, and social commentary.

The books read that year were:

- *The True Story of the Twelve Qing Emperors* (《正说清朝十二帝》) — picked up after a graduate school entrance exam in which the subject incorrectly answered that the Qing dynasty had ten emperors.
- *The World is Different Because of You* (《世界因你不同》) by Li Kaifu — found to be insubstantial upon reflection.
- *Youth* (《青春》) by Han Han — noted for the author's independent thinking and willingness to take stands.
- *What the Heck is History* (《历史是个什么玩意儿1》) — read with the motto "study history to become wiser."
- *My Struggle* (《我的奋斗》) by Luo Yonghao — valued for its emphasis on principles and reading, though the autobiographical sections were judged poorly written.
- *Where is My Hometown* (《乡关何处》) — most of the content was later forgotten, but the book reinforced the subject's ideals.

## 2013 Reading List

The subject's 2013 reading volume increased significantly over the previous year. The thirteen books covered systems programming, mobile development, web frameworks, interview preparation, and popular science.

Notable technical titles included [[The Self-Cultivation of a Programmer]], a classic on C compilation, linking, and runtime mechanics that the subject initially studied for cross-platform compilation work but later forgot through disuse. [[Waves at the Peak]] by Wu Jun traced the histories of major Silicon Valley companies. [[Beauty of Mathematics]], also by Wu Jun, explained natural-language-processing algorithms with enough clarity that the subject considered changing their graduation thesis topic around it.

The subject also read *Building Facebook* (《打造Facebook》), an account by a Chinese engineer who witnessed the company's explosive growth; *Sword Refers to Offer* (《剑指Offer》), a programming interview guide; and *Learning Android*, an introductory text later earmarked for graduation project work.

In literature and memoir, [[Ming Dynasty Those Things]] dominated the subject's reading during a six-month internship at IBM and was described as the book that influenced them most that year. The subject also read *1980s Love* (《1980年代的爱情》), a romance novel by Ye Fu, and *Take It Slow, Everything Is in Time* (《慢慢来，一切都来得及》), a motivational title consulted during stressful periods.

## Motivation and Reflection

The subject framed the 2012 reading as catching up on lost time. Earlier attempts at reading had focused on motivational titles such as *The Way of Talking*, which failed to hold interest. The subject concluded that self-help books were not a good fit.

A key realization emerged while reviewing the year's choices: the subject had been searching for their own reflection in the pages, seeking to better understand themselves through the authors' experiences.
