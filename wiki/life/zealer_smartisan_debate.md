# Zealer Smartisan Debate

In August 2014, a public confrontation took place between Luo Yonghao (founder of Smartisan Technology) and Wang Ziru (founder of Zealer) over a Zealer review of the Smartisan T1 smartphone. The debate was broadcast live on Youku and attracted significant attention within the Chinese tech community.

## Background

Zealer had published a critical review of the Smartisan T1, which Luo took issue with. Luo prepared extensively before responding, eventually challenging Wang to a face-to-face confrontation. The live broadcast devolved from a product discussion into personal attacks, with Luo accusing Wang of conflicts of interest due to investments from Xiaomi and OPPO.

## Subject's Assessment

The subject expressed dislike for both participants. Regarding Wang Ziru, the subject felt that Zealer's transition from a casual tech talk show to a startup introduced compromises that affected its perceived impartiality. The subject noted that while Wang's videos remained informative, they carried "something that shouldn't be there."

Regarding Luo Yonghao, the subject admired his unconventional path but grew disillusioned by his tendency to resolve practical problems (such as production delays) through lengthy social-media statements and verbal sparring rather than action. The subject concluded that Luo's debating skills won him fans but did little to improve product delivery or brand trust.

## Outcome

The subject predicted that Wang's credibility would be damaged in the short term, yet Zealer's dominant position in the Chinese tech-review ecosystem meant the platform would likely survive. The central issue, the subject argued, was not the quality of the Smartisan phone but Wang Ziru's personal integrity—a framing that shifted the debate away from product evaluation.
